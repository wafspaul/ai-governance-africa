## ILO Data — Kenya Employment 2022
**File:** `data_raw/ilo/ILO_Data.csv`
**Source:** ILO Continuous Household Survey, Kenya

### The headline numbers

| Group | Workers (thousands) |
|---|---|
| Total employed (15+) | 17,079,814 |
| Working-age employed (15–64) | 15,959,460 |
| Youth employed (15–24) | 2,164,824 |
| Adults employed (25+) | 14,914,990 |

### The education breakdown — this is where it gets interesting

**All workers (15+) by education level:**
| Education level | Workers | % of total |
|---|---|---|
| Less than basic | 1,373,497 | 8% |
| Basic | 7,254,597 | 42% |
| Intermediate | 7,456,648 | 44% |
| Advanced | 927,934 | 5% |

**By ISCED level (more detailed):**
| Education | Workers |
|---|---|
| No schooling | 1,352,052 |
| Primary (ISCED 1) | 1,928,664 |
| Lower secondary (ISCED 2) | 5,325,934 |
| **Upper secondary (ISCED 3)** | **7,284,028** |
| Post-secondary non-tertiary | 172,620 |
| Bachelor's degree | 840,916 |
| Master's degree | 79,482 |

**The number that matters most for this essay:**
Upper secondary (ISCED 3) is the single largest education group — **7.28 million workers, 43% of all employed Kenyans**. This is the BPO-adjacent cohort: enough education for data entry, customer service, and routine digital work — but not enough specialisation to be protected from AI substitution.

### Youth workers (15–24) education breakdown

| Education | Youth workers |
|---|---|
| Less than basic | 71,444 |
| Basic | 691,221 |
| **Intermediate (upper secondary)** | **1,307,709** |
| Advanced | 83,442 |

**Key observation:** 60% of young Kenyan workers (15-24) are at intermediate/upper secondary level — the exact education band most exposed to AI automation of routine tasks. These are workers who invested in enough education to enter the formal digital economy, and are now most at risk from it.

### The gender gap in advanced education

- Male workers with advanced education: **612,547**
- Female workers with advanced education: **315,387**

Women hold roughly half the advanced education credentials that men do. But women are overrepresented in BPO and data entry work. This suggests women may face both higher exposure to displacement AND lower cushion from education.

### What the data doesn't tell me

- Which specific JOBS people hold (sector, type of work) — the data is education-based, not occupation-based
- What share of intermediate-education workers are in AI-exposed roles vs. protected ones (farming vs. BPO)
- What happened to workers who lost jobs in previous years — no longitudinal data here
- Informal sector scale — Kenya's informal economy employs a huge additional share not fully captured
- Regional breakdown — Nairobi vs. rural Kenya will be very different stories

## Policy Engagement Log

### Senate Submission — 5 May 2026
**Bill:** The Artificial Intelligence Bill, 2026 (Senate Bills No. 4 of 2026)
**Channel:** Individual petition to Parliament of Kenya
**Clause targeted:** Section 33 (Workforce Impact Obligations)
**Core argument:** Section 33 requires workforce impact assessments but assumes formal employment relationships. 86% of Kenya's workforce is informal. The 7.5 million workers most exposed to AI displacement (Intermediate band, 46–50% informality) have no employer to trigger the assessment, no registration to access reskilling, and no channel to report displacement. Three amendments proposed: (1) expand "workforce impact" definition to include informal and platform workers; (2) require Commissioner's guidelines under Section 33(2) to include informal sector indicators; (3) add a labour market specialist with informal economy expertise to the Advisory Committee under Section 17.
**Also submitted to:** EdTech East Africa joint stakeholder submission (Area 4: Workforce Impact & Reskilling) — coordination body for Joint Memorandum to Clerk of Senate.
**Status:** Submitted. Senate cutoff was 5 May 2026.
**Relevance to essay:** The three amendments are the Section 4 governance recommendations in condensed form. This submission is proof of work and a policy engagement data point for fellowship applications.
