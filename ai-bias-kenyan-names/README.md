# AI Bias in Kenyan Names

**Sub-project of:** AI Governance Africa
**Status:** Dataset compiled. Analysis in progress.

---

## What this is

An investigation into naming bias in AI systems — specifically whether AI models trained predominantly on Western data systematically disadvantage users with Kenyan or broader African names.

The concern: if AI systems used in hiring, credit scoring, or service access perform differently for users with names like *Wafula*, *Achieng*, or *Muthoni* compared to names like *James* or *Emma*, that is a fairness failure with direct governance implications for AI deployment in Kenya and across Sub-Saharan Africa.

---

## Why it matters for governance

Naming bias is an entry point to a broader argument: AI systems exported to African markets carry the biases of the data environments they were built in. Governance frameworks that do not require bias auditing before deployment cannot detect or correct this. The Kenya National AI Strategy 2025–2030 names inclusion as a cross-cutting priority — bias in name-based decisions is one concrete, testable instance of what that inclusion claim has to contend with.
