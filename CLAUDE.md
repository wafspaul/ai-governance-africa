## Paul's Voice Rules (Anti-AI Writing Cheat Sheet)

Follow these strictly in all writing. See Notion for the full guide.

- No banned vocabulary: delve, pivotal, nuanced, robust, leverage, unlock, crucial, critical, transformative, ecosystem, synergy, seamlessly, groundbreaking
- Limit em dashes — max one per paragraph

- No hedging: "it seems", "it appears", "one might argue" — commit to what you know
- No -ing tails: "making it difficult" → "it is difficult"
- Prose over bullets in analysis — use "some things include: x, y, z" not bullet lists
- No unearned emphasis (bold only when genuinely critical)
- Use is/are not was/were for standing facts

## Key Essay Arguments (do not drift from these)

1. **The displacement risk is real and concentrated.** 7.5M workers (43.7% of workforce) sit at upper secondary (Intermediate) — the band most exposed to AI task substitution. 60% of youth workers are in this band. This is the BPO corridor cohort.

2. **Informality is the governance failure, not a footnote.** 86% of Kenya's workforce is informal. Formal governance mechanisms (labour law, retraining, social protection) reach 14%. When informal workers are displaced by automation, no system registers the loss. Invisibility is the problem.

3. **Education does not protect you.** Informality falls from ~70% (below-basic) to 26% (Advanced) — but ~half the Intermediate band is still informal. The BPO narrative assumes formal contracts. The data says that assumption is wrong for the majority.

4. **Earnings gap = no buffer.** ~40,000 KES/month gap between Intermediate and Advanced workers. Median lower than average inside the band. No savings. No severance. Displacement is a household crisis, not a career disruption.

5. **Gender and youth multiply the risk.** Women overrepresented in Intermediate, underrepresented in Advanced. Female youth: highest unemployment rate of any cohort. A gender-neutral response protects men more by design.

6. **The governance pathway.** AI displacement erodes institution legitimacy in the same institutions that carry the AI governance mandate. When those institutions can't reach 86% of the population, they lose the credibility to steer AI deployment. That is the systemic risk — not just displacement, but displacement in a context where governance cannot see it happening.
