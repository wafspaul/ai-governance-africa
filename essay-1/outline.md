## Section 1: The Labour Market AI Is Entering (~300 words)

**The question this section needs to answer:** What does Kenya's labour market actually look like, and which parts of it are most exposed to AI automation?

**What it needs to do:**
- Use real data (ILO, World Bank)
- Identify the sectors most at risk: BPO, data services, routine manufacturing, informal services
- Be specific — avoid generic claims about "AI automating jobs"

**[YOUR ANSWER — what's your specific claim here?]**

*Think about: The BPO sector in Kenya employs tens of thousands in routine tasks (data entry, customer service) that large language models can now replicate. What's the approximate number? What's the trend?*

**Real data points ready to use — pick the ones that support your argument:**

- Kenya has **17 million employed workers** (ILO, 2022) — this is the population at stake
- **7.28 million workers (43%)** hold upper secondary education as their highest qualification — the single largest group, and the one most concentrated in routine digital and service work
- **2.16 million youth workers (15–24)** are employed — of whom **1.3 million (60%)** are at intermediate education level, squarely in the most AI-exposed band
- Lower secondary + upper secondary combined = **12.6 million workers = 74% of Kenya's workforce** — this is the scale of potential exposure if AI automation spreads through routine tasks
- **Women hold roughly half the advanced education credentials that men do**, yet are overrepresented in BPO roles — suggesting they face higher exposure with less educational buffer

*Note: the ILO data tells us education levels, not occupation sectors. You should acknowledge this limitation — it strengthens your credibility.*
